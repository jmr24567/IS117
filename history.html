History of Matcha

Discover the roots of this vibrant green tea.

The Origins of Matcha

Matcha has a rich history that dates back to ancient China during the Tang Dynasty (618–907 AD), where tea leaves were steamed and formed into bricks for easy transport and trade.

Matcha in Japan

It wasn't until the 12th century that a Zen monk named Eisai brought powdered tea to Japan. This tea evolved into what we know today as Matcha and became deeply embedded in Japanese culture.

The Art of the Tea Ceremony

The Japanese tea ceremony, or "chanoyu," emphasizes mindfulness, respect, and tradition. Matcha is at the heart of this ceremonial practice.

“Tea is more than just a drink. It’s a way of life.”

Modern Matcha

Today, Matcha is enjoyed worldwide in everything from lattes to desserts—appreciated both for its taste and health benefits.
